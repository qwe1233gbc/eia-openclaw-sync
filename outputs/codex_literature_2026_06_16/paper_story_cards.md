# Paper Story Cards - Codex Draft 2026-06-16

## Card 1 - Meyur et al. 2025

**Paper:** *Benchmarking LLMs for Environmental Review and Permitting*

**Scientific question:** Can LLMs perform specialized regulatory reasoning over environmental review/permitting documents?

**Method:** Builds NEPAQuAD from EIS documents and evaluates models with MAPLE under zero-shot and context-driven settings.

**Useful thesis sentence:** Prior EIA/permit LLM work shows that domain-specific environmental review tasks require benchmark construction from real regulatory documents, and that performance depends strongly on the quality of retrieved or supplied context.

**How this thesis differs:** Instead of a broad NEPA QA benchmark, this thesis builds a small but domain-bounded Chinese report-table audit benchmark for Foshan plastic-sector VOCs/composite-process review.

## Card 2 - Cilliers et al. 2025

**Paper:** *Large Language Model-assisted EIA screening: a case study using GPT*

**Scientific question:** Can a customized GPT model support EIA screening under list-based legal thresholds?

**Method:** Iteratively develops GPT screening versions and tests against 20 South African EIA cases.

**Useful thesis sentence:** LLMs can assist EIA workflows when customized to local legal contexts, but complex projects and multiple triggers still require human oversight and iterative refinement.

**How this thesis differs:** Screening identifies whether EIA is required; this project examines technical review of report content, evidence, standards, and reviewer-style follow-up questions.

## Card 3 - Gimenez et al. 2025

**Paper:** *Facilitating public scrutiny of EIA reports with open data and artificial intelligence*

**Scientific question:** Can AI and open data make EIA reports more transparent and easier to scrutinize?

**Method:** Uses open data, AI-supported assessment, and comparative impact significance analysis in a Mexican EIA case.

**Useful thesis sentence:** AI-supported EIA review can be framed not as replacement of expert judgment, but as a way to improve traceability, expose uncertainty, and support scrutiny under limited procedural time.

**How this thesis differs:** The thesis operationalizes traceability through explicit standard cards and evidence-linked experience cards.

## Card 4 - Garrido & Requena 2011

**Paper:** *Proposal of ontology for environmental impact assessment*

**Scientific question:** How can heterogeneous EIA terminology and knowledge be structured for reuse?

**Method:** Proposes an EIA ontology and expert-facing knowledge mobilization interface.

**Useful thesis sentence:** EIA intelligent systems need explicit domain knowledge structures because environmental terms, methods, and evidence requirements vary by context.

**How this thesis differs:** Instead of constructing a universal EIA ontology, the thesis builds a pragmatic standard-card library for one bounded review scenario.

## Card 5 - Watson & Marir 1994

**Paper:** *Case-based reasoning: a review*

**Scientific question:** How can previous cases support reasoning without a complete explicit rule model?

**Method:** Reviews CBR as solving new problems by adapting prior successful solutions or cases.

**Useful thesis sentence:** Class-case experience can be understood as a case-based reasoning resource: it does not replace rules, but helps identify likely omissions and reviewer concerns in similar contexts.

**How this thesis differs:** This thesis adds evidence grading and relevance filtering to prevent unbounded transfer of prior-case experience.

## Card 6 - Zhao et al. 2023

**Paper:** *ExpeL: LLM agents are experiential learners*

**Scientific question:** Can LLM agents learn from accumulated task experience without fine-tuning?

**Method:** Extracts natural-language insights from training tasks and recalls them during inference.

**Useful thesis sentence:** Natural-language experience can be externalized and reused by LLM agents, supporting the representation of review comments as experience cards.

**How this thesis differs:** The experience is not self-generated by an agent; it is curated from real EIA review comments and constrained by evidence levels.

## Card 7 - Allard et al. 2026

**Paper:** *Experiential Reflective Learning for Self-Improving LLM Agents*

**Scientific question:** How should agents retrieve task-relevant heuristics from accumulated experience?

**Method:** Reflects over trajectories to create heuristics and retrieves top relevant heuristics at test time.

**Useful thesis sentence:** Experience is useful only when selectively retrieved; indiscriminate insertion of all prior experience creates scalability and relevance risks.

**How this thesis differs:** The thesis implements relevance rules by task type, module, industry/process match, and evidence level.

## Card 8 - Du 2026

**Paper:** *Memory for Autonomous LLM Agents*

**Scientific question:** How is agent memory written, managed, read, and evaluated?

**Method:** Survey of memory mechanisms and evaluation from 2022 to early 2026.

**Useful thesis sentence:** The experience library can be framed as managed long-term memory: it must include write-path filtering, contradiction handling, selective recall, and evaluation.

**How this thesis differs:** This project is not building an autonomous memory system; it is a curated review-memory layer for assisted EIA review.

## Card 9 - Hogan et al. 2022

**Paper:** *Knowledge Graphs*

**Scientific question:** How can structured knowledge be represented, queried, validated, and extracted?

**Method:** Comprehensive survey of KG models, query/validation languages, and extraction methods.

**Useful thesis sentence:** Standard cards are an intermediate structured-knowledge representation: less formal than a full KG, but already more traceable than free-form prompt context.

**How this thesis differs:** The current artifact is a card library/JSONL library, not a complete graph database.

## Card 10 - Fragiadakis et al. 2025

**Paper:** *Evaluating Human-AI Collaboration*

**Scientific question:** How should human-AI collaboration be evaluated beyond raw task accuracy?

**Method:** Reviews HAIC evaluation and proposes a framework integrating quantitative and qualitative metrics.

**Useful thesis sentence:** EIA intelligent review should be evaluated not only by answer correctness, but also by evidence use, actionability, knowledge grounding, and safe collaboration with human reviewers.

**How this thesis differs:** v0.6a has a single-researcher rubric and lacks independent double scoring; this is a future validation task.

## Card 11 - Alon-Barkat & Busuioc 2023

**Paper:** *Human-AI interactions in public sector decision making*

**Scientific question:** Do public decision-makers over-rely on algorithmic advice or selectively adhere to it?

**Method:** Experimental studies with citizens and civil servants.

**Useful thesis sentence:** Public-sector AI must be designed as decision support because algorithmic advice can interact with human biases and institutional context.

**How this thesis differs:** The thesis does not test human reviewer behavior; it uses this literature to justify cautious deployment claims.

## Card 12 - Lee et al.; Kabir et al.

**Paper cluster:** EIS/environmental statement quality review

**Scientific question:** How can EIS quality be reviewed and what recurring quality gaps exist?

**Method:** Review packages and quality assessment studies.

**Useful thesis sentence:** EIA review has an established quality-assessment tradition, which this thesis translates into machine-readable benchmark items and scoring dimensions.

**How this thesis differs:** This project narrows quality review into standard-card and experience-card assisted audit questions for a specific industry guide.
